It is the manipulation technique that exploits human psychology and truck people into revealing confidential informations.

## 1. Common Types

### 1.1. Phishing

It happens where attackers send frauludent emails, text messages (smishing), or make phone calls (vishing) that appear to be from legitimate sources to steal data or install malware.

## 1.2. Pretexting

The attacker creates an invented scenario or "pretext" to gain a victim's trust and extract information. This involves impersonating an authority figure, such as an IT support or high-leval executive.

## 1.3. Bainting

Attackers lute victims with a false promise or desirable offer, like a free download or a malware-infected USB drive left in a public place, to access systems or information.

## 1.4 Qui Pro Quo

An attacker promises a service or benefit (e.g., free IT assistance) in exchance for sensisitve information like login credentials.

## 1.5. Scareware

Victims are bombarded with alarming, fake messages about security threats (e.g., "Yout computer is infected with a virus!") to pressure them into installing malicious software or paying a ransom.

## 1.6. Tailgating

A physical security breach where an unauthorized person follows an authorized individual into a restricted area, often by exploiting social courtesy (e.g., asking someone to hold the door).

## 1.7. Business Email Compromise (BEC)

A targeted attack where an attacker compromises os spoofs an executive's email account to trick an employee into performing an unauthorized action, such as a wire transfer of funds.
